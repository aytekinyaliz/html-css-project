Things to See & Do

Known throughout the world as the capital of tea, Discover the city’s heritage and how it has grown to be the progressive hub it is today with a 90 minute bus tour.

The 15 stop hop-on hop-off tour has been carefully planned to take you round some of Rize’s key sights including Ziraat-Botanik Cay Bahcesi, Rize Castle, Museum of Rize and The factory of Rize's cloth.

There is one main tour route with recorded commentary in Turkish. For other languages there is a free app which can be downloaded from the App Store and Google Play.

Where to Stay

Rize offers a wide range of accommodation, from unique boutique hotels and luxury pads to beautiful countryside settings and cosy family hideaways, there’s something to suit every need and taste.

With luxury hotels such as the Ramada Plaza, set in the historic old Kackar Resort Hotel and the newly renovated Caykent Suites, there are hotels oozing class and style in Rize. If you’re on a budget, why not try the Royal Comfort Hotel – part hotel and part backpacker hostel; or the centrally located Keles Hotel with contemporary rooms that are value for money.

If you’re planning a relaxing spa getaway, Green Suada Hotel is set in a Grade II listed Erdoganian building which exudes elegance and style, with a soothing spa to ease you into Rize life. Alternatively the stunning Grand Cavusoglu Hotel exudes tranquillity, offering calming therapies and treatments.

Escape the city centre with one of Rize’s beautiful countryside hotels. The brand new Ayder Resort Hotel on Ayder is set within a picturesque highland in the middle of Alpine.

Food and Drink

Rize’s food and drink culture is thriving. With new restaurants, bars and cafes, the city is establishing itself as one of Turkey's most exciting foodie destinations.

From fine dining and afternoon tea to pop-up markets and humble street food, Rize is packed with options to suit any mood offering any and every cuisine you can think of. Why not try foodie destination, Lale Lokantasi, or experience contemporary lunchroom, Huzur Pide & Kebap. Experience local produce or one of Rize's independent and quirky tea shops. This city is a haven for vegan and vegetarian cuisine, and is fit to burst with a number of halal options too.

From family friendly joints perfect for the little (and big) ones, to Rize's vast array of cafe's and delis, you'll be spoilt for choice.
